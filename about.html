About the Lab

The ALLI_GATORS Lab is dedicated to understanding the fundamental mechanisms of kidney and cardiovascular physiology, with a focus on epithelial ion transport, extracellular vesicle biology, and lipid signaling. Our research bridges basic science and clinical applications to develop new therapeutic strategies for hypertension, kidney disease, and cardiovascular disorders.

Core Research Areas

  • Ion Channel Regulation: ENaC, sodium chloride cotransporters, and epithelial transport mechanisms
  • Hypertension & Blood Pressure: Salt-sensitive hypertension, circadian regulation, and therapeutic interventions
  • Extracellular Vesicles: Urinary exosomes as biomarkers and mediators of kidney disease
  • Lipid Biology: Bioactive lipids, membrane microdomains, and lipid signaling pathways
  • Kidney Disease: Diabetic nephropathy, chronic kidney disease, nephrotic syndrome, and kidney stones
  • Cardiovascular Health: Endothelial function, vascular biology, and coarctation of the aorta

Key Molecular Targets

  • MARCKS and MARCKS-like proteins
  • Protein kinase C signaling
  • Cathepsin proteases
  • Alpha-1 antitrypsin
  • Circadian clock proteins (Per1, BMAL1)
  • Metabolic regulators (metformin, dapagliflozin, tempol)

Our Approach

We employ cutting-edge techniques including lipidomics, proteomics, electrophysiology, and advanced imaging to investigate disease mechanisms at the molecular and cellular levels. Our work integrates in vitro cell culture models, ex vivo tissue preparations, and in vivo animal models (including diabetic, hypertensive, and genetically modified mice) to translate findings from bench to bedside.

Clinical Relevance

Our research directly impacts human health by:

  • Identifying novel biomarkers in urinary extracellular vesicles for early disease detection
  • Elucidating mechanisms of diabetes-associated hypertension and diabetic kidney disease
  • Discovering therapeutic targets for salt-sensitive hypertension
  • Understanding cardiovascular complications including COVID-19 effects on the heart and kidneys
  • Investigating kidney stone formation and ferroptosis in renal pathology

Collaborative Science

We maintain active collaborations with researchers at the University of Florida, Medical College of Wisconsin, University of Texas, and other leading institutions. Our interdisciplinary approach brings together expertise in physiology, biochemistry, lipidomics, proteomics, and clinical medicine to address complex biomedical questions.

Funding & Support

Our research is supported by grants from the National Institutes of Health (NIH), American Heart Association, and other funding agencies. We are committed to rigorous, reproducible science and openly share our findings through peer-reviewed publications and scientific presentations.
